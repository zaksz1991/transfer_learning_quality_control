Automated Quality Control with Transfer LearningProject OverviewThis project leverages Transfer Learning to automate the detection of 6 distinct surface defects in a manufacturing context. By using a pre-trained ResNet-50 model, we have built a vision pipeline that standardizes image inputs, extracts high-level visual features, and classifies product quality in real-time.Data Preparation & PreprocessingStandardization: Images are resized to $224 \times 224$ to match ResNet-50 input requirements, ensuring consistency in feature extraction.Data Augmentation: Techniques including rotation and horizontal flipping were implemented to artificially expand the dataset and improve model robustness against orientation variations on the factory line.Technical ImplementationModel Architecture: We utilized include_top=False to strip the original classification layers of ResNet-50 while retaining the powerful feature-extraction capabilities of the base model.Architectural Rationale: We chose GlobalAveragePooling2D over Flatten to minimize the parameter count. This significantly reduces the risk of overfitting, which is critical when working with limited industrial datasets, and maintains spatial awareness of the defect patterns.Action & Performance ReflectionDecision Logic: The model outputs a probability score for each class. Our factory automation logic triggers a "Rejection" only when the model’s confidence exceeds $\geq 85\%$.Threshold Tuning: A high confidence threshold is enforced to minimize False Positives (incorrectly rejecting good products), ensuring the efficiency of the production line while maintaining high-quality standards.Limitations: The current model achieved ~51% accuracy on validation. Future iterations would benefit from "fine-tuning" (unfreezing the upper layers of ResNet) and increasing the training duration to improve precision on more subtle defect classes.
